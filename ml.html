Evaluation of Categorization Models

This may seem backward, but before training a ML model it's a good idea to have a handle on how you are going to evaluate it. The confusion matrix is fundamental to understanding a model's performance.

The name 'confusion matrix' is a good name, because it can be remarkably confusing. There are many ways to add, subtract and divide the cells in the table that give quite different measures of performance. Unfortunately many of these measures have multiple names that mean the same thing. For example what is commonly expressed in medicine as specificity is also known as selectivity or true negative rate . Check out https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sensitivity_and_specificity for a list with formula.

One important thing to consider is which metric is most important to you. Commonly only accuracy is reported, but this single number can be quite misleading. For example imagine you have tested positive for a rare form of cancer (occuring in 1 in 10000 people or 0.1% incidence) and you are told that the test is 99% accurate and has 99% sensitivity and 99% specificity. What would you think the chance of the test being wrong and you actually don't have the cancer (a false positive)?

The answer is 91%!
